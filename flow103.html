Landing Pages

A landing page is usually a one paged website that ideally focuses on a particular event or brand. It's major focus is to create awareness.

Recap: We discussed portfolio websites as well as the rules that guide creating a good one.

What are Landing Pages?

A landing page is used for driving awareness to an event, brand, idea or movement. When compared to a portfolio, a landing page brings about awareness of something while a portfolio website is used to showcase the services a brand offers.

When discussing with your client about the kind of site they're looking for, it's important to be able to differentiate between the two. If your client needs a site for an event going on in the community, it's ideally going to be a landing page but if a business owner needs a website for showcasing their meals, that's likely going to be a portfolio.

It's pertinent to note that landing pages are usually very engaging and as such, images and videos are used majorly to bring out the central idea of the page

Key things to remember when building websites

Brand Color

In a landing page or any project you're building, it's very important to never deviate from the color's of the brand. 

Client

If your client is a single individual or a brand that does one thing, a single page site will suffice but a company website may require multi pages and may even need legal documents!

Font

Although we haven't talked a lot about fonts, understand that for some level of visual aesthetics, you may need to decorate the font of some texts on websites or even change the entire font scheme of the website.

Niche

If you're building a website for a place of worship, school or hospital and the theme is black, you're making a mistake. Certain colors are meant for certain types of websites.

Block format

Mobirise offers a lot of blocks and you may have to improvise and use a block for something other than what it was initially meant for. That's very OK just ensure you apply caution in adding blocks.

Challenge

Click any text to edit or style it. Select text to insert a link. Click blue "Gear" icon in the top right corner to hide/show buttons, text, title and change the block background.
